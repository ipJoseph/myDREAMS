1. Purpose & Pipeline Alignment

Mission: Systematically contact as many leads as possible during each Power Hour session to identify qualified buyers, build relationships, and fill the sales pipeline.

This SOP governs the QUALIFY phase of our sales pipeline — converting raw leads into qualified buyers with clear requirements and intent.

QUALIFY
→
CURATE
→
CLOSE
→
NURTURE

Every conversation during Power Hour should move a lead further down this pipeline. The primary outcomes we are working toward on each call:

  • Appointment set — Best outcome. Face-to-face or video meeting scheduled.
  • Requirements captured — We know what they want (area, price, timeline).
  • Email obtained — Added to drip campaign for ongoing nurture.
  • Status clarified — Not ready, working with someone, or no longer interested.

2. Pre-Session Setup

Arrive at your workstation by 8:55 AM. Complete all setup before the 9:00 AM start so you can begin dialing immediately.

  • Log into Follow Up Boss (FUB)
  • Open SmartLists in separate browser tabs:
    • Priority 1 New Leads
    • Priority 2 Brand New Leads
    • Priority 3 Hand Raised
    • Priority 4 Warm Pond
  • Launch the FUB dialer (test audio if needed)
  • Open a notepad for quick call notes
  • Silence phone notifications and close unrelated tabs
  • Review any overnight lead activity in the myDREAMS dashboard
Pro Tip
Check the myDREAMS priority call list before starting — it surfaces leads ranked by heat score, recent activity, and engagement signals that may not appear in FUB SmartLists.

Pacing Rule
Do not spend more than 3 minutes on any single lead unless they are actively engaged in conversation. If you are talking, keep talking. But do not linger on research, notes, or unanswered calls.

4. Call List Priority Order

Work these lists in order. Do not skip around or cherry-pick. Discipline in list order ensures no lead falls through the cracks.

Priority SmartList Description Approach
P1 New Leads Fresh inquiries from the last 24–48 hours. Highest conversion potential. Speed-to-lead is critical. Be the first agent they talk to. Direct and enthusiastic.
P2 Brand New Leads Leads from the past week with no contact yet. Still warm. Move quickly through the list. First contact establishes position.
P3 Hand Raised Leads who clicked, responded to a campaign, or showed behavioral interest. Reference their specific activity. “I noticed you were looking at homes in [area]…”
P4 Warm Pond Past contacts, sphere, previously nurtured leads. Relationship-first. Check in, provide value, ask for referrals.
myDREAMS Integration
The myDREAMS scoring system surfaces leads by Heat (IDX activity), Value (revenue potential), and Relationship (communication frequency). Cross-reference the myDREAMS priority call list with FUB SmartLists to catch high-intent leads that may not appear in standard CRM filters.

5. Call Handling Workflow

5.1 Making the Call

  1. Dial using FUB dialer only. Never dial manually — all calls must be logged in the CRM automatically.
  2. Let it ring 5–6 times. Give them a chance to pick up. Don’t hang up early.
  3. Follow the appropriate path below based on the outcome.

5.2 Call Outcomes

If They Answer

  1. Follow the Conversation Framework (Section 6)
  2. Qualify: timeline, motivation, area, budget, financing
  3. Log concise notes immediately after the call
  4. Set a follow-up task if appropriate
  5. Claim the lead (see Section 8 for claiming rules)
  6. Move to next call

If You Reach Voicemail

Voicemail Script
“Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] with Jon Tharp Homes. I wanted to reach out and connect with you about real estate in the [area] area. Give me a call back when you get a chance — my number is [Your Number]. Have a great day!”
Keep it under 20 seconds. Warm, professional, no pressure.
  • Log as “Left Voicemail” in FUB
  • Move to next call immediately

If No Answer (No Voicemail)

  • Log as “No Answer” in FUB
  • Move to next call immediately

6. Conversation Framework & Scripts

These scripts are frameworks, not word-for-word recitations. The goal is natural conversation. Adapt your tone and language to match the person you are speaking with. Be yourself — be genuine.

6.1 Opening — Build Comfort

You
“Hey, is this [Mr./Mrs. Name]?”
Wait for confirmation. Use their name correctly.
You
“Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] with Jon Tharp Homes. How are you doing today?”
Let them answer. Be present and listen.
You
“That’s great to hear. Just enjoying this beautiful weather we’ve been having — can’t beat it, right?”
Keep it real. Match the weather, the season, whatever feels natural. 15–20 seconds of small talk.

6.2 Transition — Open the Door

Approach A: Open-Ended (Recommended)
“Well look, I don’t want to take up too much of your time, but I was calling to see if there was anything in the world I could do for you?”
Disarming. Most people don’t expect this question. Let the silence work for you.
Approach B: Direct
“I was calling because you recently [inquired about / expressed interest in] real estate in [Area]. Wanted to see how I could help you with that.”
More direct. Best for leads who took a specific action (property inquiry, form fill).

6.3 Qualify — Gather Requirements

Ask these questions conversationally, not as an interrogation. Weave them into the dialogue naturally.

Category Question What You’re Learning
Intent “Are you currently looking to buy, sell, or both?” Lead type classification
Timeline “What’s your timeline looking like?” Urgency and heat level
Location “What area are you focused on?” Geographic requirements for property matching
Budget “Do you have a price range in mind?” Value score, property search parameters
Financing “Have you been pre-approved yet, or is that something I can help with?” Readiness level; opportunity to add value
Blockers “What’s stopped you from [buying/selling] so far?” Objections to address, obstacles to solve

6.4 Build Rapport & Close

Build Rapport
“That’s exciting! I’m curious — when were you planning on interviewing an agent like myself for the job of [selling your home / helping you find the right place]?”
Frames you as a professional being hired, not a salesperson chasing. Confident but not pushy.
Close: Set Appointment
“I’d love to help however I can. Can we set up a time to meet this week and go over what you’re looking for?”
Always aim for a specific day/time. “This week” creates urgency.
Close: Send Properties
“I’d love to send you some properties that match what you’re describing. What’s the best email to send those to?”
Getting an email = a win. Add to drip campaign and property alerts.
If They’re Not Ready
“No problem at all! I completely understand. I’d be happy to send you some options in the meantime so you can get a feel for what’s out there. What’s the best email for you?”
Never burn a bridge. Email capture keeps the lead alive for nurture campaigns.

7. Objection Handling

Objections are not rejections — they are requests for more information. Respond with empathy first, then redirect.

Objection Response
“I’m already working with an agent.” “That’s great! I hope they’re taking good care of you. If anything changes or you ever want a second opinion, don’t hesitate to reach out.” Log it. Move on. Don’t compete.
“I’m just browsing / not ready yet.” “Totally understand! A lot of my clients start the same way. I’d love to send you some listings so you can see what’s out there. What’s a good email?”
“How did you get my number?” “You [registered on our website / inquired about a property / filled out a form]. I just wanted to follow up and see if I could help.” Be transparent and calm.
“I can’t talk right now.” “No worries! When would be a better time for a quick call?” Schedule the callback and log it immediately.
“Take me off your list.” “Absolutely, I’ll take care of that right now. I apologize for the inconvenience.” Remove immediately. Mark as Do Not Call in FUB. Comply promptly and professionally.

8. Lead Claiming Rules

Clear, consistent rules for lead ownership prevent conflicts and ensure fair distribution.

Rule Details
Claiming Trigger A lead is claimed only when you make actual contact (live conversation). Leaving a voicemail or getting no answer does not constitute a claim.
Morning Hold New leads may not be claimed before 12:00 PM unless it is your assigned shift. This ensures fair access across the team.
Response Position If a lead responds to a previous agent’s outreach (call, text, or email), the original contacting agent holds position one. Honor system applies.
After Claiming Update the lead’s stage in FUB, assign to yourself, and update your Super Saved Search in the CRM.
Integrity Standard
Lead claiming operates on the honor system. When in doubt, defer to the agent who made the first outreach attempt. Disputes should be escalated to the team lead promptly.

9. Post-Session Workflow

Complete these steps immediately after your 12:00 PM session end. Budget 15–20 minutes for post-session wrap-up.

  1. Enrich your notes. Go back through every conversation from the session. Add detail, context, and next steps while it’s fresh.
  2. Set follow-up tasks. Every hot lead should have a dated follow-up task in FUB. No lead should leave the session without a next action.
  3. Update lead stages. Move any lead you made contact with to the appropriate FUB stage (Prospect, Active Buyer, etc.).
  4. Claim your leads. Assign leads you connected with to yourself in FUB.
  5. Update Super Saved Search. Add new buyer criteria to your CRM saved searches for matching.
  6. Submit your scorecard. Send your daily numbers to the team lead (calls made, conversations, appointments, follow-ups).
myDREAMS Workflow
After Power Hour, capture buyer requirements from your conversations into the myDREAMS intake form. This feeds the automated property matching system — your qualified buyers will automatically receive matched property alerts.

11. Weekly & Monthly Review

Weekly Review (Friday)

  • Total calls, conversations, and appointments for the week
  • Conversion rates vs. benchmarks
  • Follow-up tasks completed vs. scheduled
  • Leads claimed and stages updated
  • Top 3 hottest leads to prioritize next week

Monthly Review (1st of Month)

  • Month-over-month trends in all KPIs
  • Pipeline health: leads in each stage
  • SmartList accuracy — are the right leads surfacing?
  • Script effectiveness — what’s working, what needs adjustment?
  • Team-wide lead claiming and distribution review

Power Hour Cheat Sheet

Session Rules

  • Volume + Speed + Consistency = Results
