● This is a significant evolution. Let me think through what you're describing.

  The Shift

  wncmountain.homes says: "I'm a real estate site for Western NC."

  wncmountains.com says: "I'm the guide to Western North Carolina."

  That's not a rebrand. That's a repositioning. Real estate goes from being the product to being one destination within a
  lifestyle ecosystem. And .com carries more authority than .homes for a platform play.

  The Subdomain Architecture

  wncmountains.com          → The front door. "Find Your Dream."
  hiking.wncmountains.com   → Trail guides, difficulty ratings, seasonal tips
  food.wncmountains.com     → Restaurants, farmer's markets, farm-to-table
  music.wncmountains.com    → Venues, festivals, local scene
  art.wncmountains.com      → Galleries, studios, art walks
  homes.wncmountains.com    → The real estate search (current public site)
  wellness.wncmountains.com → Spas, yoga, medical, spiritual
  outdoors.wncmountains.com → Golf, fishing, kayaking, climbing
  antiques.wncmountains.com → Shops, markets, dealers

  Why This Is Powerful

  1. The funnel inverts. People don't search "homes in Asheville." They search "best hiking near Asheville" or "farm to
  table Black Mountain." Those searches land on your content. Your content introduces the place. The place introduces the
  homes. You own every step.

  2. SEO becomes a moat. Each subdomain builds topical authority in its category. hiking.wncmountains.com ranking for "WNC
  hiking trails" is achievable. Zillow will never compete for that keyword. You're playing in a space they can't enter.

  3. Behavioral data gets rich. Someone who reads 5 articles on hiking.wncmountains.com, then visits food.wncmountains.com
  for Black Mountain restaurants, then lands on homes.wncmountains.com, tells you more about themselves than any intake form
   ever could. You know their lifestyle before they ever give you their name.

  4. The brand becomes bigger than real estate. "WNC Mountains" is a destination brand, not an agent brand. That scales
  beyond you. If you ever bring on team members, each could own a subdomain's content. If you ever sell the business, you're
   selling a media property, not just a book of clients.

  What I'd Push Back On (gently)

  Subdomains vs paths. There's an SEO tradeoff. wncmountains.com/hiking shares domain authority with the root.
  hiking.wncmountains.com builds its own authority independently. Subdomains are better for distinct brands/apps. Paths are
  better for content that reinforces one domain's authority.

  My recommendation: start with paths (wncmountains.com/hiking, wncmountains.com/food) to build the root domain's authority
  first. Move to subdomains later if a category grows large enough to stand alone. Google treats subdomains as semi-separate
   sites; that's powerful when you have depth, but dilutive when you're starting out.

  Don't boil the ocean. 8 subdomains at launch means 8 content properties to maintain. Start with 2-3 that align with your
  strongest knowledge and the highest search volume. Hiking and food are probably the top two for WNC. Homes is already
  built. Everything else can roll out over time.

  The Campaign Layer

  "Find Your Dream" ties it all together. Each category page can end with:

  ▎ "Ready to make the mountains home? See what's available in [area they've been reading about]."

  That's not a CTA. That's a natural next step for someone who's already emotionally invested. The transition from content
  to listings feels like service, not sales.

  Technical Reality Check

  The current public site (Next.js at wncmountain.homes) would become homes.wncmountains.com with minimal code changes
  (mostly DNS and Next.js config). The new root wncmountains.com would be a separate lightweight site, likely a landing page
   that routes into the subdomains/paths. The content sites (hiking, food, etc.) could be Notion-powered with a simple
  renderer, or a shared Next.js app with route-based content.

  This is buildable with what we have. Not overnight, but the architecture supports it.
